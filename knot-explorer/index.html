Skein Relations

Alexander Polynomial \(\Delta(q)\)

$$\Delta_{L_+}(q) - \Delta_{L_-}(q) = (q^{1/2} - q^{-1/2})\,\Delta_{L_0}(q)$$

Normalized so \(\Delta(\text{unknot}) = 1\). Invariant under mutation. Plotted in variable \(q\).

Jones Polynomial \(V(q)\)

$$q^{-1} V_{L_+}(q) - q\, V_{L_-}(q) = (q^{1/2} - q^{-1/2})\, V_{L_0}(q)$$

Discovered by V. Jones (1984). Distinguishes trefoil from unknot and detects chirality.

HOMFLY-PT \(\mathfrak{sl}(3)\) fund.

$$q^{3}\, P_{L_+} - q^{-3}\, P_{L_-} = (q - q^{-1})\, P_{L_0}$$

Quantum \(\mathfrak{sl}(3)\) invariant, fundamental representation \(\square\). The \(A_2\) Reshetikhin–Turaev invariant.

HOMFLY-PT \(\mathfrak{sl}(4)\) fund.

$$q^{4}\, P_{L_+} - q^{-4}\, P_{L_-} = (q - q^{-1})\, P_{L_0}$$

Quantum \(\mathfrak{sl}(4)\) invariant, fundamental representation \(\square\). The \(A_3\) Reshetikhin–Turaev invariant.

Cube of Resolutions

For each crossing in a knot/link diagram, compute the 0-smoothing (A-resolution) and 1-smoothing (B-resolution). The 2n vertices of the n-cube correspond to all possible resolution choices. Vertices connected by edges differ by exactly one crossing resolution.

Sign & smoothing conventions

Crossing sign. Stand on the under-strand facing its direction of travel. If the over-strand passes from your right to your left the crossing is +1; left to right is −1. Equivalently, with planar tangent vectors, \( \varepsilon = \operatorname{sign}\bigl(\det[\,\mathbf{u}_{\text{under}},\,\mathbf{u}_{\text{over}}\,]\bigr) \).

PD labelling. A crossing is recorded as X[a, b, c, d] where arcs are listed counterclockwise starting from the incoming under-arc: a = incoming under, c = outgoing under, and b, d are the two over-arcs with the over-strand oriented b → d iff the crossing is positive.

Resolutions. Each crossing is replaced by one of two non-crossing arc pairs:

  • 0-smoothing (A): pair a↔d and b↔c. The two new arcs sit on opposite sides of the over-strand's line (they don't cross it).
  • 1-smoothing (B): pair a↔b and c↔d. The two new arcs sit on opposite sides of the under-strand's line.

Kauffman A/B regions. Rotate the over-strand 90° counterclockwise: the two regions it sweeps into are the A-regions. The 0-smoothing joins the A-regions; the 1-smoothing joins the B-regions.
